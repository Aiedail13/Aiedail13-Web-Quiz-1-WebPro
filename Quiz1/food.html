What is Cireng?

Cireng which stands for "aci digoreng" (english : fried tapioca flour) is one of the most famous street food in Indonesia. Just like the name, cireng is made from tapioca flour as the main ingredients. Apart from the main ingredients, the fritters consist of various additions that typically include garlic, scallions, and different seasonings, while modern-day varieties also incorporate meat or sausages. Cireng fritters are characterized by their round shape, pale color, and a crispy exterior, while the center remains soft and chewy.

What is Seblak?

Seblak Seblak has a savory and spicy taste. It made from wet crackers cooked with vegetables and protein sources like eggs, chicken, seafood or even beef. One last main inggredient is kencur. Seblak is now a really popular street food that liked by lot of Indonesian people. This chewy texture food has a spicy and refreshing taste, and has several variations. How to make seblak is by soak them into the hot boiled water and add spices and seasoning, such as garlic, onions, salt, kencur, etc.

What is Mie Kocok?

Mie Kocok Mie kocok is an aromatic soup that combines sliced beef, beef offal, or meatballs with flat yellow noodles and other accompaniments such as crackers, bean sprouts, sliced scallions, and fried shallots. All ingredients are served in a clear beef broth, while spicy sambal paste and soy sauce may be served as condiments. The name mie kocok roughly translates as shaken noodles, it is because the noodles are shaken in a strainer before they are added to the soup.
