Situ Patenggang Lake, Bandung, Indonesia

Hometown: Bandung

Bumi Pasundan lahir ketika Tuhan sedang tersenyum.

M.A.W.Brouwer

Bandung is the capital of West Java province. The city surrounded by volcanic mountain and because of that Bandung has mild, pleasent and cooler year-round temperatures than most other Indonesian cities. Back in colonial times, Bandung resemblance Paris and European atmosphere and Dutch often called Bandung as Parijs van Java (Paris of Java). The remaining of the European atmosphere can be felt through art-deco style on lot of old architectural building in Bandung. For the example is Gedung Sate. It was used as Dutch Indies Government Companies. The roof has a decoration symbolizing 6 million guldens, the cost of the building. The decoration has a shape of popular satay food as now it is famous for the name. Interestingly, this building mixes different architectural styles: Spanish Moorish style for the windows, Italian renaissance for the over all building, and between Balinese pura & Thai's pagoda for the roof.


Aside from it, just around Bandung's city limits we can find lush greenery from hillsides that covered in tea plantations. There are short drives up to the mountain resorts of Lembang and the Bosscha Observatory, and higher to the volcanic crater of Tangkuban Perahu, Kawah Putih, the only crater in Java accessible all the way by car as far as its rim. 7km away from Kawah Putih, we can find Situ Patenggang Lake. One of the most beautiful lake in West Java. In the city hub, you'll find its commercial and business districts are alive with dining spots, cafes and boutique-lined streets. Basically we can find factory outlets to expansive shopping malls. Overall, you almost can find everything in Bandung. We can find cafes to factory outlets. Museum, old towns and buildings and even natural tourism like mountain craters, forests and waterfalls. It makes Bandung as one of the top vacation destinations in Indonesia.
